# NFL MVP Analysis - 2025 Season

## Executive Summary

The 2025 NFL MVP race features compelling candidates across all positions, but the numbers point to a clear winner. While traditional volume-based metrics favor Matthew Stafford and non-QB candidates, advanced analytics reveal **Drake Maye** as the season's most valuable player.

**My Pick to Win:** Matthew Stafford (LA Rams)
**Who Should Win:** Drake Maye (New England Patriots)

---

## The Case for Drake Maye (Should Win)

### Historic Efficiency on a Rebuilding Team

Drake Maye leads the NFL with **0.298 EPA per play** - a generational level of efficiency rarely seen from rookies, let alone on struggling teams. He's carrying the Patriots' offense single-handedly:

**2025 Stats:**
- **0.298 EPA/play** - #1 in NFL (25% better than 2nd place)
- **31 touchdowns**, 4,195 yards
- **65.6% completion rate**
- **+10.8 CPOE** - Best accuracy over expected
- **50% deep ball completion rate** - Historic mark

**Why He Deserves It:**

1. **Unprecedented Efficiency**: His 0.298 EPA/play would rank in the top 5 single-season marks of the analytics era. For context, Patrick Mahomes' MVP season was 0.34, and Maye is approaching that on a far worse team.

2. **Historic Rookie Performance**: No rookie QB has ever posted this combination of volume (540 attempts) and efficiency. He's rewriting what's possible for first-year players.

3. **Team Context**: The Patriots have limited weapons and a shaky offensive line. Maye is creating value in spite of his circumstances, not because of them. This is the essence of "most valuable."

4. **Deep Ball Mastery**: His 50% completion rate on 20+ air yard throws is unprecedented. Elite veterans rarely crack 45%. He's completing deep balls at a historic rate while maintaining overall efficiency.

5. **Success Rate**: 56% success rate means he's advancing the ball and keeping drives alive more consistently than any other QB in football.

**The Analytics Verdict:** Maye is the NFL's most efficient quarterback on a per-play basis, creating more value per snap than any player in football.

---

## The Case for Matthew Stafford (Will Win)

### Volume, Touchdowns, and the Narrative

While Maye has the efficiency edge, **Matthew Stafford will likely win the MVP** because he has the traditional stats voters love:

**2025 Stats:**
- **46 touchdowns** - Most in NFL (15 more than Maye)
- **4,557 passing yards** - #2 in NFL
- **620 attempts** - Most volume among elite QBs
- **0.227 EPA/play** - #3 in NFL
- **Leading the Rams offense** - Team success matters

**Why He'll Win:**

1. **Touchdown Machine**: 46 touchdowns is an elite season total. Voters love TDs, and Stafford has nearly 50% more than Maye.

2. **Volume + Efficiency Combo**: He's not just chucking it - 0.227 EPA/play is elite while leading the league in attempts. That's durability + effectiveness.

3. **The Narrative**: Veteran QB leading a resurgent Rams team with his arm strength and deep ball prowess. Voters love a comeback story.

4. **Deep Ball King**: League leader in deep yards (1,257) and deep attempts (90). He's the NFL's premier vertical threat, and spectacular plays get votes.

5. **Name Recognition**: Stafford is a known quantity having a career year. Voters are more comfortable giving the award to a proven veteran than a rookie, even a historic one.

**The Reality:** MVP voters prioritize volume stats (TDs, yards) and team success over per-play efficiency. Stafford checks all the traditional boxes.

## Other Top Contenders

### Quarterbacks

**Jordan Love (GB) - 0.239 EPA/play**
- Strong efficiency (2nd in NFL) with 23 TDs, 3,216 yards
- Lacks the volume for serious MVP consideration
- Packers' success helps his case, but stats don't stand out enough

**Brock Purdy (SF) - 0.194 EPA/play**
- Elite efficiency on limited volume (298 attempts)
- "System QB" narrative hurts his MVP case
- 49ers use committee approach, limiting his workload

**Jared Goff (DET) - 0.173 EPA/play**
- 34 TDs, 4,305 yards - solid volume numbers
- Detroit's elite run game means he's not "carrying" the offense
- Good season, but not MVP-level efficiency

**Dak Prescott (DAL) - 0.168 EPA/play**
- 30 TDs, 4,350 yards - good but not great
- Lacks the efficiency or volume to stand out
- Cowboys' team success could boost his case marginally

### Running Backs

**Bijan Robinson (ATL) - 2,300 scrimmage yards, 12 TDs**
- League leader in total yards from scrimmage
- Workhorse back with 367 touches
- Lacks TD production for MVP consideration (12 TDs vs Taylor's 20)

**Jonathan Taylor (IND) - 1,965 yards, 20 TDs**
- Best TD total among RBs, elite EPA/rush (0.058)
- Complete back: rushing + receiving threat
- Strong efficiency + volume combo

**Christian McCaffrey (SF) - 2,106 yards, 17 TDs**
- Second in scrimmage yards, elite all-around back
- 413 touches shows he's the centerpiece of SF offense
- Injury concerns earlier in career might affect voting

**Derrick Henry (BAL) - 1,745 yards, 16 TDs**
- Elite efficiency (0.030 EPA/rush)
- At age 30+, still one of the league's best
- Ravens' committee approach limits his case

**Why No RB Will Win:**

The last non-QB MVP was Adrian Peterson in 2012 (2,097 rushing yards). Modern NFL is pass-heavy, and EPA heavily favors QBs. Even elite RB seasons (2,300 yards) can't match the EPA impact of efficient QB play. A running back would need 2,500+ yards or 25+ TDs to seriously challenge.

### Wide Receivers

**Jaxon Smith-Njigba (SEA) - 1,793 yards, 10 TDs**
- League leader in receiving yards
- Elite EPA/target (0.556) for a WR1
- 119 receptions shows consistent production

**Puka Nacua (LA) - 1,715 yards, 11 TDs**
- Second in yards, highest WR EPA/target (0.697)
- 129 receptions - workhorse receiver
- Benefits from Stafford's deep ball prowess

**George Pickens (DAL) - 1,431 yards, 9 TDs**
- Big-play ability (0.643 EPA/target)
- Moved to Dallas and elevated his game
- Lacks the volume/TDs for serious consideration

**Why No WR Will Win:**

No wide receiver has ever won MVP. Jerry Rice's best seasons didn't get it. Cooper Kupp's historic 2021 (145/1,947/16) didn't get it. Even a 1,800-yard season won't overcome QB bias. WRs are too dependent on QB play to be considered "most valuable."

## MVP Value Comparison: Total EPA

To objectively compare across positions, we calculate **Total EPA Added** (EPA per play × total plays):

**Top 5 by Total EPA:**

1. **Drake Maye (QB)** - 161.0 total EPA (0.298 × 540 attempts)
2. **Matthew Stafford (QB)** - 140.7 total EPA (0.227 × 620 attempts)
3. **Jordan Love (QB)** - 110.4 total EPA (0.239 × 462 attempts)
4. **Jared Goff (QB)** - 106.6 total EPA (0.173 × 616 attempts)
5. **Dak Prescott (QB)** - 106.5 total EPA (0.168 × 634 attempts)

**Best Non-QBs:**
- **Jonathan Taylor (RB)** - 21.5 total EPA (0.058 × 370 touches)
- **Puka Nacua (WR)** - 115.7 total EPA (0.697 × 166 targets)

**The EPA Reality:**

Drake Maye has added **20 more expected points** than any other player in football. In a close game, those 20 points could be 2-3 additional wins. That's literal "value."

Even the best RB (Taylor at 21.5 EPA) can't approach elite QB production. WR Puka Nacua has impressive EPA, but much of that is derivative of Stafford's performance - they can't both claim credit.

## The Verdict

### Who **Should** Win: Drake Maye

By every advanced metric that matters - EPA/play, CPOE, success rate, efficiency-adjusted impact - Drake Maye is having a historically great season. He's the NFL's most efficient QB while playing on one of its worst rosters. His 0.298 EPA/play would rank in the top 20 single-season marks of the analytics era.

Maye is creating value at an unprecedented rate for a rookie and doing it without elite weapons or protection. This is textbook "most valuable player" - the one player whose individual performance is adding the most wins to his team.

**Analytics-based verdict: Drake Maye MVP**

---

### Who **Will** Win: Matthew Stafford

But this isn't a pure analytics vote. MVP voters are influenced by:
- **Volume stats**: 46 TDs and 4,557 yards look incredible
- **Narrative**: Veteran having a career year, leading Rams offense
- **Highlight plays**: Deep ball king with spectacular throws weekly
- **Team success**: Rams likely in playoffs, Patriots likely not
- **Voter comfort**: Proven veteran vs. unproven rookie

History shows MVP voters love QBs with elite TD totals and big volume. Stafford has both. Voters are also hesitant to give the award to rookies, even historic ones. The last rookie QB to get serious MVP consideration was RG3 in 2012, and he didn't win.

**Prediction: Stafford wins 60-40 in voting**

## Conclusion

The 2025 MVP race highlights the tension between **analytics** (which favor Maye's historic efficiency) and **traditional voting** (which favors Stafford's volume and narrative).

**Drake Maye is having a better season** by every efficiency metric. He's creating more value per play than anyone in the NFL and doing it on a roster that provides little help. This is the definition of "most valuable."

**Matthew Stafford will likely win** because he has the touchdown totals, yardage, and veteran narrative that MVP voters traditionally reward. His 46 TDs and 4,557 yards are elite marks that look incredible on paper.

If I had a vote, I'd choose Maye. But I'm predicting Stafford takes home the hardware.
